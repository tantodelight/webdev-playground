Jollof rice is a beloved West African dish made with rice cooked in a flavorful blend of tomatoes, onions, peppers, and spices. Known for its rich red color and smoky taste, it's often served with fried plantains, chicken, or beef ‚Äî a true celebration food across Nigeria and beyond.


Ingredients:
  • 1 cup uncooked long-grain rice
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 large tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 Scotch bonnet peppers, chopped (optional)

Instructions:
  • Heat oil in a large pot over medium heat.
  • Add onion, ginger, cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper. Cook until onion is translucent.
  • Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they break down and the mixture thickens.
  • Add rice to the pot, stirring to coat with the tomato mixture.
  • Add water to the pot and bring to a boil.
  • Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 20-25 minutes or until rice is cooked.
