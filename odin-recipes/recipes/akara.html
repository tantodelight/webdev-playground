Akara, also known as bean cake, is a popular Nigerian street food made from blended black-eyed peas, onions, and spices, then deep-fried until golden and crispy. Crunchy on the outside and soft inside, it's often enjoyed with pap or bread for breakfast


Ingredients:
  • 1 cup dried black-eyed peas or brown beans
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions:
  1. Soak beans in water overnight. Drain and blend into a smooth paste.
  2. In a large bowl, combine bean paste, onion, garlic, ginger, salt, black pepper, and cayenne pepper (if using). Mix well.
  3. Using your hands, shape the mixture into small balls or patties.
  4. Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium-high heat.
  5. Fry akara until golden brown and crispy, about 3-4 minutes per side. Drain on paper towels
